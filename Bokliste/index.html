My booklist

This is a list of all the books I've read since september 2017, when I decided to turn my life around. In addition to beeing great sources of knowledge, they also inspired me to program this list to further improve my JavaScript abilities.

Through reading, people can change their own lives for the better and shape their own futures. Help create a literate world through books and technology.

Sass for web designers

by Dan Cederholm
categoryNon-fiction
released2013
pages098
levelMedium
score5
read03.18
Amazon

A short and easy introduction to SASS for Web Designers. I used it mainly as a repetition after learning about SASS a couple of motnhs ago.

It's well written and contains a lot of exaples on basic and some advanced consepts. It really made me wish I had used SASS for this project. That would make it so much easier.

Recommended as an introduction or as repetition.

Meditations (Dover Thrift Editions)

by Marcus Aurelius
categoryNon-fiction
released1998
pages112
levelMedium
score5
read02.18
Amazon

From Amazon:
Stirring reflections on the human condition from a warrior and emperor provide a fascinating glimpse into the mind and personality of a highly principled Roman of the 2nd century. Recognizing that suffering is at the core of life, he counsels stoic detachment in the face of inevitable pain, loss and death.

It is absolutely fascinating to be able to read the thoughts and philosophy of one of the great Roman emperors. He seems like a very wise man. And it's obvious to me that this book is the inspiration and source behond many other books, quotes and life philosophy.
